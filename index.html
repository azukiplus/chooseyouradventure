The room is silent except the soft taps of your favorite pen against the cherry wood desk. *tap, tap, tap... * Out the blinds, you notice the rain pouring so hard that it feels like the earth is crying. A sudden shrill ring of the phone interrupts your thoughts.

You pick up the phone and a scratchy, panicked voice greets you. For a reason unbeknownst to you, a feeling of despair fills you to the brim. "I've got a case for you," he shouts, panting and out of breath. Your head starts to hurt. You have a premonition that something is about to go very wrong.

You let the phone keep ringing. Something deep inside you tells you that you need to pick up the phone. Why don't I try picking up the phone?

Minutes later, the phone stops ringing and only the sound of you tapping your pen remains. tap, tap, tap... You close your eyes, starting to drift off peacefully... when the shrill screech of the phone ringing once again blasts your unsuspecting ears.

"What do you mean, "What do you mean?" the voice hisses at you. "You're a detective, aren't you? I want you to solve the murder case of my son."

Your finger trembles on the *end call* button, but you can't bring yourself to press the button.

You end the call to get some medications. As you get up, your foot gets caught, and you fall to the ground and crumple into a pile. You start to shiver.

Using all of your power, you slowly point your body back towards the phone that you put down. On all fours, you crawl forward one step at a time. Your headache worsens from moving, and your hand gets stuck on what you tripped on-- a rusted, large metal pipe.

"Thank you," the man whispers, and ends the call. You get up to start your investigation, but crumple to the ground the second you stand up. Your headache is unbearable. You start to lose consciousness.
